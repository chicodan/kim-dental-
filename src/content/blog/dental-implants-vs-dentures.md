If you're missing one or more teeth, you have more options than ever before — and choosing between them can feel overwhelming. The two most common tooth-replacement solutions are **dental implants** and **dentures**, and while both restore your smile, they work in very different ways.

Here's an honest comparison to help you make the best decision for your health, your lifestyle, and your budget.

## What Are Dental Implants?

A dental implant is a titanium post surgically placed into the jawbone, acting as an artificial tooth root. Once the implant fuses with the bone (a process called osseointegration), a crown is attached on top. The result looks, feels, and functions exactly like a natural tooth.

Implants can replace a single tooth, several teeth (using a bridge), or all your teeth (with an implant-supported full arch restoration).

## What Are Dentures?

Dentures are removable appliances — either full (replacing all teeth) or partial (replacing several teeth) — that rest on the gum line. Modern dentures are much more natural-looking than they used to be, but they still require adhesive and come out at night.

## Side-by-Side Comparison

| | **Implants** | **Dentures** |
|---|---|---|
| **Feel** | Like natural teeth | Can shift or slip |
| **Eating** | No restrictions | Some foods difficult |
| **Bone health** | Preserves jawbone | Bone loss continues |
| **Maintenance** | Brush & floss normally | Remove, soak nightly |
| **Lifespan** | 20+ years (often lifetime) | 5–8 years before relining |
| **Cost** | Higher upfront | Lower upfront |
| **Surgery required** | Yes | No |

## The Hidden Cost of Dentures

Dentures may seem more affordable at first, but they require frequent adjustments and replacements as your jaw shape changes over time. And because dentures don't stimulate the jawbone the way natural teeth do, bone loss can continue after tooth removal — leading to a sunken facial appearance and ill-fitting dentures.

Implants, on the other hand, actively preserve bone. They're a long-term investment that many patients find worth every penny.

## When Dentures Make More Sense

Dentures are a valid option when:
- A patient is not a good surgical candidate due to health conditions
- Significant bone loss has already occurred (though bone grafting may still enable implants)
- Budget is the primary constraint

## When Implants Are the Superior Choice

For most healthy adults, implants offer significant advantages:
- **No slipping** — implants are fixed and permanent
- **Eat what you love** — including apples, steak, and corn on the cob
- **Speak naturally** — no worrying about dentures shifting mid-sentence
- **Bone preservation** — your facial structure stays intact
- **Self-confidence** — many patients say implants changed their life

## What About Cost?

Implants do cost more upfront, but many insurance plans now cover a portion of implant treatment, and our office offers flexible payment options. When you account for the longevity of implants and the ongoing maintenance costs of dentures, implants often become the more economical choice over time.
