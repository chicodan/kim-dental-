## What Makes a Good Implant Candidate?

Most healthy adults who are missing one or more teeth are candidates for dental implants. The key requirements are:

- **Adequate jawbone density** — the implant needs bone to fuse with. If bone loss has occurred, a bone graft procedure may be performed first.
- **Healthy gums** — active gum disease must be treated before implant placement.
- **Good overall health** — certain conditions (uncontrolled diabetes, bleeding disorders) may affect healing. Dr. Kim will review your full health history at your consultation.
- **Non-smoker or willingness to quit** — smoking significantly reduces implant success rates.

Age is not a disqualifier. Dr. Kim has placed implants for patients in their 70s and 80s with excellent results.

## The Implant Process at Kim Dental

**Step 1 — Consultation and Planning**
We take digital X-rays and often a cone-beam CT scan to evaluate your bone structure. Dr. Kim creates a customized treatment plan and discusses all options, including cost and timeline.

**Step 2 — Implant Placement**
The titanium post is placed under local anesthesia. Most patients report the procedure was far less uncomfortable than they anticipated. The area is numbed thoroughly, and you'll feel pressure rather than pain.

**Step 3 — Osseointegration**
Over 3–6 months, the implant fuses with your jawbone. During this healing period, you'll have a temporary restoration if needed.

**Step 4 — Abutment and Crown**
Once the implant is fully integrated, an abutment connects the post to your custom crown. The crown is color-matched and shaped to blend seamlessly with your surrounding teeth.

**Step 5 — Aftercare**
Implants are cared for exactly like natural teeth — brush, floss, and come in for regular cleanings. With proper care, implants can last a lifetime.
