When you think about protecting your smile, your gums deserve just as much attention as your teeth. Gum disease is one of the most common oral health conditions we see at our Paramus practice, affecting nearly half of all adults over 30. The good news? When caught early, gum disease is highly treatable—and often reversible. Understanding the warning signs can help you take action before minor irritation becomes a more serious concern.

## What Is Gum Disease and Why Does It Matter?

**Gum disease**, also called **periodontal disease**, is an infection of the tissues that support your teeth. It starts when plaque—a sticky film of bacteria—builds up along and beneath the gumline. If not removed through regular brushing, flossing, and professional cleanings, this plaque hardens into tartar, which can only be removed by a dental professional.

Left untreated, gum disease doesn't just affect your mouth. Research has linked advanced periodontal disease to other health concerns, including heart disease, diabetes complications, and respiratory issues. Taking care of your gums is truly an investment in your overall wellness.

## Early Warning Signs to Watch For

Your gums often give you signals when something isn't right. Being aware of these early indicators can make all the difference:

- **Bleeding gums** when brushing or flossing (healthy gums shouldn't bleed regularly)
- **Red, swollen, or tender gums** instead of a firm, pink appearance
- **Persistent bad breath** that doesn't improve with brushing
- **Receding gums** that make your teeth look longer than before
- **Loose teeth** or changes in how your bite fits together

Many people dismiss bleeding gums as normal, but this is often the first sign that your gums need attention. If you notice any of these symptoms, don't wait—early intervention leads to much better outcomes.

## Understanding the Stages of Gum Disease

**Gingivitis** is the earliest stage and the only one that's fully reversible. At this point, inflammation is limited to the gums, and there's no damage to the bone or connective tissue. With improved oral hygiene and professional cleaning, gingivitis can often be resolved completely.

When gingivitis progresses, it becomes **periodontitis**. This stage involves the formation of pockets between your teeth and gums, where bacteria can accumulate and cause deeper infection. The bone and fibers supporting your teeth begin to break down, which can eventually lead to tooth loss if not addressed.

**Advanced periodontitis** represents the most serious stage, where significant bone loss has occurred and teeth may become loose or require extraction.

## Treatment Options That Work

The treatment Dr. Kim recommends will depend on how far the condition has progressed. For gingivitis, a thorough professional cleaning combined with better home care is often all that's needed.

For periodontitis, **scaling and root planing** is a common treatment. This deep cleaning procedure removes plaque and tartar from below the gumline and smooths the tooth roots to help gums reattach. In more advanced cases, surgical treatments or referral to a periodontist may be necessary.

**Here's something you can do today:** Commit to flossing every night before bed. Many patients here in Bergen County tell us they brush faithfully but skip flossing—yet cleaning between your teeth removes bacteria that brushing simply can't reach. This single habit can significantly reduce your risk of gum disease.

## Protecting Your Smile for the Long Term

Prevention truly is the best medicine when it comes to gum health. Regular dental checkups allow us to catch problems early, when treatment is simplest and most effective. Consistent brushing, daily flossing, and avoiding tobacco products all contribute to healthier gums.
