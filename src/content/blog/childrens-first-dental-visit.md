Taking your child to the dentist for the first time is a milestone worth celebrating! While it's natural to feel a little nervous—both for you and your little one—knowing what to expect can make the experience positive and even fun. At Kim Dental, we love welcoming young patients and helping them build healthy habits that last a lifetime.

## When Should Your Child's First Visit Happen?

The **American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry** recommends that children see a dentist by their first birthday or within six months of their first tooth appearing—whichever comes first. This might seem early, but there's good reason behind it. Baby teeth are susceptible to cavities as soon as they emerge, and early visits help us catch any potential issues before they become problems.

These initial appointments are also about building familiarity. When children visit the dentist regularly from a young age, they're far less likely to develop dental anxiety later in life. Think of it as laying the groundwork for a lifetime of healthy smiles.

## What Happens During the First Visit?

Your child's first dental appointment is designed to be gentle, brief, and stress-free. Here's what typically happens:

**A friendly introduction.** We take time to let your child get comfortable in our office. For very young children, the exam might even take place with your little one sitting on your lap—whatever helps them feel safe.

**A gentle examination.** Dr. Kim will carefully check your child's teeth, gums, and jaw development. We're looking at how teeth are coming in, checking for any signs of early decay, and making sure everything is developing normally.

**A simple cleaning.** If your child is old enough and comfortable, we may do a light cleaning to remove any buildup. For babies and toddlers, this might simply involve wiping the teeth with a soft cloth.

**Parent education.** This is your chance to ask questions! We'll discuss proper brushing techniques for little teeth, when to introduce flossing, nutrition tips for healthy teeth, and habits to watch for (like thumb-sucking or pacifier use).

## How to Prepare Your Child for Success

A little preparation at home can make a big difference in how your child feels about their first visit. Here are some practical ways to set them up for success:

**Keep it positive.** Avoid using words like "hurt," "shot," or "pain" when talking about the dentist. Instead, focus on how the dentist helps keep their smile bright and healthy.

**Read books or watch videos.** There are wonderful children's books about visiting the dentist that can familiarize your child with what to expect. Seeing their favorite characters have positive dental experiences can be reassuring.

**Play pretend.** Practice "going to the dentist" at home by counting each other's teeth or using a toothbrush to "check" a stuffed animal's smile. This makes the real visit feel less unfamiliar.

**Schedule wisely.** Book your appointment for a time when your child is typically well-rested and fed. A cranky, hungry toddler will have a harder time, no matter how gentle we are!

**Pro tip you can use today:** Start getting your child comfortable with having their mouth examined by gently lifting their lip to look at their teeth during regular brushing. This simple practice helps them accept the dentist doing the same thing.
