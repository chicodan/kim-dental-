Few phrases in dentistry cause more anxiety than "you need a root canal." But here's the truth: **the procedure itself is no more uncomfortable than getting a routine filling.** The pain you associate with root canals is actually the infection that made the root canal necessary — not the treatment.

At Kim Dental in Paramus, we perform root canal therapy regularly and consider it one of the most rewarding procedures we offer. Why? Because patients walk in suffering and leave relieved.

## Why Root Canals Are Necessary

Your tooth has a soft inner core called the pulp, which contains nerves and blood vessels. When bacteria reach this pulp — through a deep cavity, a cracked tooth, or repeated dental work — it becomes infected and inflamed. That infection causes the throbbing, temperature-sensitive pain many people experience.

Without treatment, the infection can spread to the jawbone and surrounding teeth. A root canal removes the infected pulp, cleans out the canals inside the root, and seals the tooth so you can keep it for life.

## Step-by-Step: What Happens During the Procedure

**1. Numbing the area**
We apply a topical gel before any injection, then administer local anesthetic. Most patients are surprised by how little they feel during this step. Once numb, you should feel no pain — just pressure.

**2. Isolating the tooth**
A small rubber dam is placed around the tooth to keep it dry and free of bacteria.

**3. Accessing the canals**
A small opening is made through the top of the tooth. We use specialized instruments to carefully remove the infected pulp tissue from each canal inside the root.

**4. Cleaning and shaping**
Each canal is cleaned, shaped, and disinfected. This is done gently and methodically — and again, you should feel nothing but occasional pressure.

**5. Filling and sealing**
The cleaned canals are filled with a biocompatible material and sealed. A temporary or permanent filling closes the access point.

**6. Crown placement**
Most teeth that undergo root canals need a crown to protect and restore full function. This is typically done at a follow-up appointment.

## After the Procedure

Some mild soreness for 2–3 days is normal and easily managed with over-the-counter pain relievers. Most patients return to normal activities the very next day.

## Signs You Might Need a Root Canal

- Persistent toothache, especially when chewing
- Prolonged sensitivity to hot or cold
- Darkening or discoloration of the tooth
- Swelling or tenderness in nearby gums
- A small pimple-like bump on the gum near the tooth

If you're experiencing any of these symptoms, don't wait. The sooner we treat an infected tooth, the better the outcome — and the less discomfort you'll endure.
